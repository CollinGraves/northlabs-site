The Problem

Your tech diligence provider has never set foot on a job site.

Billions are flowing into construction technology, field service software, and industrial platforms. But the firms evaluating these acquisitions are running the same playbook they'd use on a fintech or an HR tool. That's a problem.

■The Generalist Gap

Traditional tech DD firms can tell you if the code is clean. They can't tell you if the BIM integration handles IFC files correctly. Construction, trades, and industrial software operate in environments that generalist diligence providers don't understand — offline-first architectures for sites without connectivity, trade-specific workflow logic, embedded fintech with unique take-rate economics, and mobile UX designed for workers in steel-toed boots, not designers in ergonomic chairs.

■The Industry Gap

Construction consultancies can tell you about the market. They can't evaluate whether the AI is real or a demo. Industry-specialized firms bring deep commercial understanding of the built environment, but they don't perform code-level assessment, architecture evaluation, or AI capability analysis. When a target claims their AI predicts job site safety incidents, someone needs to evaluate the model, the training data, and the production deployment — not just take the pitch deck at face value.
